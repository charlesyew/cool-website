FAQ

Where were you from? Why did you choose to study abroad?

I was from Penang, a tiny island in Malaysia famous for its street food. When I grew up, I never thought of leaving my hometown. In fact, I didn't even know what Singapore and the United States was like until I was about nine or ten years old (though I did memorize the location of the countries on the world map when I was five). I was fortunate enough to meet a group of friends who share the same interest in math, and together we participated in primary school math olympiad competitions. The questions felt more like math puzzle than theory back then so it was mostly just us having fun as kids. We did well enough to qualify for the invitational round of a regional competition, received a scholarship from Hwa Chong after the competition, and the rest is history.

Why did you decide to study quant finance?

Looking at my CV, it should be quite apparent that quant finance wasn't really always at the top of my radar. In fact, when I chose my undergraduate degree, I wanted to be an investment banker. I saw the prestige that the profession brings, and convinced myself that I'd be a good fit. However, in university, I started gaining exposure to other career paths, and saw different possibilities for myself. After trying out a few of those, I realized that while I was decent in those fields, I was never the best. That was when I made up my mind that I should try to build my career around my passion for applied math. After all, if everything goes south, at least I can still teach math for a living. I crafted a 10 year career roadmap, which led me to where I am now, with 8 1/2 years more to go.
(Also, all of my family members are engineers, so perhaps this is the closest I can get to become an engineer.)

Which books do you recommend for quant finance?

I am perhaps not the most qualified person out there to give book recommendations, but here are some of the nicer books that I read to get myself up to speed with the technicalities in the quant field:

  • A First Course in Probability by Sheldon Ross Completed
  • Econometrics by Bruce Hansen Completed
  • Forecasting: Principles and Practice by Rob Hyndman and George Athanasopoulos Completed
  • Introduction to Linear Algebra by Gilbert Strang Completed
  • A Primer for the Mathematics of Financial Engineering by Dan Stefanica Completed
  • Understanding Analysis by Stephen Abbott Reading
  • Monte Carlo Methods in Financial Engineering by Paul Glasserman Reading
  • Microeconomic Theory by Mas-Colell, Whinston & Green Completed
  • Advanced Macroeconomics by David Romer Completed
  • Options, Futures & Other Derivatives by John Hull Completed
  • Accelerated C++ by Andrew Koenig & Barbara Moo Reading

Why do you like to study stochastic models?

I have always liked understanding random occurances. I am someone who likes pattern a lot, so when I see randomness in game environments and real world systems, I feel compelled to dig into that whole mess and find out if there are indeed underlying mechanisms that govern such enigmatic patterns. It feels kind of like magic doesn't it? :)

What do you like to do besides studying?

I like to read for fun as well! So exciting I know. My favorite books are the Murakami ones, like 1Q84 & Kafka on the Shore. I like strategy games a lot too, so games like chess, poker & bridge are my go-to when I have spare time. I used to play badminton on an inter-school level, but now I am probably only fit for amateur games...
